## Where You Stand

For a 66-year-old male, your HbA1c of 7.6% puts you **above the ADA target of <7%** and in roughly the **worst-controlled ~30%** of managed T2D patients. Your CGM shows a clear pattern: a predictable **dawn phenomenon** (glucose climbs 05:00-06:30 every single day, peaking ~190-210) and **large postprandial spikes** (routinely 220-270) especially after evening meals containing bread/pasta/rice + coated peanuts or pizza. Your fasting glucose of 170 suggests overnight/basal control is inadequate. On better days (Nov 29, Dec 1, Dec 3 afternoon) when you eat fish, vegetables, and smaller carb portions, you stay 110-160 for hours — proof your metabolism can do much better with the right inputs.

## The 2 Things to Change

1. **Eliminate the evening carb stacking.** Your worst spikes (240-270) consistently follow dinners combining starchy base (pita, pasta, rice) + a second carb source (coated peanuts, pizza, pastels, bread with butter). Swap to one modest portion of your bulgur majadra or sweet potato (~100-120g) paired with protein and vegetables — meals like your tilapia + veggie days, which kept you under 160.

2. **Address the dawn phenomenon with your physician.** You spike to ~190-210 between 5-7 AM every single day regardless of what you ate. This is a medication/basal insulin timing issue, not a diet issue. Adjusting evening medication timing or adding/optimizing basal coverage could drop your HbA1c by 0.5% alone.
