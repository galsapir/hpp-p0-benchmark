## The Dawn Phenomenon

Every single morning between **05:00–07:00**, your glucose rises 30-50 mg/dL without food. This is classic **hepatic glucose dumping** (dawn phenomenon), prominent in T2DM. On Nov 23: 138→205 by 06:00. This happens every day in your data. **This alone may need medication adjustment** (e.g., basal insulin or evening metformin if not already on it).

## What To Do Now

1. **Cut the evening refined carbs.** Pizza, pasta, pastels, fried rice — these are your biggest glucose drivers. Swap for protein + vegetables + healthy fats. This alone could drop your mean glucose 20-30 mg/dL.

2. **Stop the coated peanuts.** They're candy. Switch to raw almonds or plain peanuts (you already eat these — your almond snacks barely register on CGM).

3. **Manage portion size on bread.** 200g of spelled bread at dinner is 64g carbs. Halve it, or replace with a lower-carb option.

4. **Talk to your doctor about the dawn phenomenon.** If you're not on metformin ER at bedtime or basal insulin, this pattern warrants it. Your fasting glucose of 170 confirms inadequate overnight coverage.

5. **Get lipids, BP, and kidney function checked** (if not recent). At your age, with your HbA1c, you should be on a statin and likely an ACE inhibitor/ARB regardless of current numbers.

6. **Keep doing what works.** Your breakfast routine (tahini + cottage cheese + oatmeal) and fish-based lunches are genuinely good. Build dinner around the same principles.
